Security

Your security is very important to us. Seriously. Very important. We understand that by using DownJS you're trusting us not only to send you the correct scripts, but to not interfere with your users in any way - malicious or not. While we have to keep our script search functionality hidden, everything else is open sourced and publically viewable on Github. Look for anything with a DownJS- prefix. That will include this website, the DownJS script, and the tracking modules as referenced below.

Tracking

We also understand that it's important that your users aren't tracked in any ways that they haven't agreed to. In the name of transparency, here's a list of things we will and will not do when it comes to tracking.

Things We Will Do

  • This public facing website is tracked using standard Google Analytics. That only applies to the http://downjs.com and http://www.downjs.com domain.
  • Our script searcher and loader is tracked with mixpanel. We never store your domain name, or any user-specific information along with these results. Each time a session is started with the script loader we assign a unique ID. This ID is only used to track the flow of that current session, so we can pinpoint bottlenecks.
  • When you upload a script to DownJS we do store user-specific details such as IP Address. This data is necessary so we can weed out people uploading malicious or spammy content.
  • We will keep this list updated, and will be intentional about informing you if anything changes.
  • We will consider you, our user, before making any changes to these policies, and will hold ourselves to the moral standard outlined on this page.

Things We Won't Do

  • We will never track your users for simply loading the DownJS script. The tracking begins once they request scripts from our servers.
  • We will never sell, publicize, or in any other way share our tracking data.
  • We will never prioritize anything higher than keep your users secure and our tracking minimal

As an additional comfort to you, any tracking we do (beyond standard Google Analytics) is open sourced and publically viewable on Github.
